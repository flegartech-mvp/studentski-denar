# PRODUCT PITCH — Študentski Denar

## One-Sentence Pitch

The no-account, no-cloud budget tracker built specifically for Slovenian university students — your money data stays in your browser, forever.

## Product Description

Študentski Denar is a browser-first personal finance app that lets Slovenian students track every euro without handing their financial data to a server. You open it, you log income and expenses, you get charts and monthly summaries — and nothing ever leaves your device. No signup. No subscription. No API key. No cloud. Just you and your money.

Built with React 19 and Tailwind CSS on a Vite stack, the app feels fast and native even though it runs entirely in the browser. Categories are customisable, monthly views give you a clear picture of where your student stipend actually goes, and spending analytics highlight patterns you didn't know existed. For power users who want to support the project, a Stripe-style signed supporter license unlocks additional features — verified locally, never phoned home.

## Best 5 Features

- **Zero-account budgeting** — no registration, no email, no password; just open and start tracking
- **100% local data storage** — all income, expenses, and categories live in your browser's local storage; nothing is ever sent to a server
- **Category management** — create, rename, and colour-code custom spending categories to match your actual life (groceries, rent, coffee, textbooks)
- **Monthly summaries + spending analytics** — visual breakdowns of where your money went, month by month, with trend lines
- **Supporter license system** — offline-verified signed license for users who want to back the project and unlock extras, no subscription required

## 30-Second Demo Flow

1. Open the app in a browser — no login screen appears
2. Click "Add Income" and enter a student stipend amount with the category "Štipendija"
3. Click "Add Expense" and log a coffee expense under "Hrana & Pijača"
4. Add two more expenses (rent, transport) so the dashboard has real data
5. Switch to the "Analitika" (Analytics) view — pie chart and bar chart populate instantly
6. Open "Mesečni pregled" (Monthly Summary) to show the balance and category breakdown
7. Point out that closing and reopening the browser still shows all the data — because it never left

## Target Audience

Slovenian university students aged 18–26 managing a student stipend, part-time job income, and shared living costs. Students who are privacy-conscious, don't want another app asking for their email, and are comfortable using a web browser as their primary tool.
